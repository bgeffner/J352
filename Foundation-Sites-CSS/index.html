An Illustrious Legacy: LeBron James Through the Years — And Counting

LeBron James' Career Arc: A Broad Overview

LeBron James — widely considered by many, including myself, to be the GOAT (Greatest of All Time) — was originally born in Akron, Ohio on December 30, 1984. He attended St. Vincent-St. Mary High School, making national headlines as a basketball sensation before being selected as the first overall pick in the 2003 NBA Draft by the Cleveland Cavaliers.

James made a myriad of contributions to his hometown team during his first stint with the Cavaliers. He won Rookie of the Year his first season, then led Cleveland to a NBA Finals appearance in 2007, where they lost to the San Antonio Spurs. In a live television special during the summer of 2010, later tabbed "The Decision," LeBron announced his intention to leave the Cavs and join the Miami Heat. Teaming up with fellow superstars Dwyane Wade and Chris Bosh was the right decision for James — the trio won titles in both 2012 and 2013. Next up came another trip back to Cleveland; James left Miami to rejoin the Cavaliers in 2014, promising to deliver a championship to the city. He fufilled it, leading the Cavs to their first-ever NBA title while delivering Cleveland's first major sports championship in over 50 years with a NBA Finals win over the Golden State Warriors in 2016. James decided to go out west two years later, signing with the Los Angeles Lakers. Once more, he helped deliver his team a championship. The Lakers won the 2020 NBA Championship, with James securing his fourth title and fourth NBA Finals MVP. James' journey isn't over yet — he still currently plays for the Lakers, even making history this past October by suiting up with his son, Bronny. It's a storied journey, life and legacy — LeBron James is certainly one of the greats. He deserves his flowers.
